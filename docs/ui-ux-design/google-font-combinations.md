# 10 Most Popular Google Font Combinations

> **Beautiful and elegant font pairings using Google Fonts.**

---

## The Top 10 Pairings

### 1. Open Sans / Prata

- **Heading:** Prata (Serif)
- **Body:** Open Sans (Sans-serif)
- Classic elegance with clean readability.

---

### 2. Oswald / Merriweather

- **Heading:** Oswald (Sans-serif, condensed)
- **Body:** Merriweather (Serif)
- Bold, impactful headlines with comfortable reading body text.

---

### 3. Playfair Display / Slabo

- **Heading:** Playfair Display (Serif, high contrast)
- **Body:** Slabo (Serif, slab)
- Editorial and sophisticated. Great for content-heavy pages.

---

### 4. Rufina / Poppins

- **Heading:** Rufina (Serif)
- **Body:** Poppins (Sans-serif, geometric)
- Warm serif headings balanced with modern geometric body text.

---

### 5. Lora / Gentium Book Basic

- **Heading:** Lora (Serif, calligraphic)
- **Body:** Gentium Book Basic (Serif)
- A serif-serif pairing that works through contrast in character -- Lora is more decorative, Gentium is more neutral.

---

### 6. Muli / Playfair Display

- **Heading:** Playfair Display (Serif, high contrast)
- **Body:** Muli (Sans-serif)
- Dramatic headings with clean, lightweight body text.

---

### 7. PT Sans / PT Serif

- **Heading:** PT Sans (Sans-serif)
- **Body:** PT Serif (Serif)
- Designed as a superfamily -- built to work together with shared proportions and rhythm.

---

### 8. Raleway / Merriweather

- **Heading:** Raleway (Sans-serif, elegant)
- **Body:** Merriweather (Serif)
- Thin, stylish headings with highly readable serif body text.

---

### 9. Bree Serif / Open Sans

- **Heading:** Bree Serif (Serif, upbeat)
- **Body:** Open Sans (Sans-serif)
- Friendly and approachable. Great for casual or playful brands.

---

### 10. Cabin / Old Standard TT

- **Heading:** Cabin (Sans-serif, humanist)
- **Body:** Old Standard TT (Serif, traditional)
- A warm, humanist sans paired with a traditional serif for a refined but accessible feel.

---

## Practical Tips for Better Font Pairing

- **Create visual contrast** -- pair fonts that look distinctly different from each other.
- **Set up hierarchy** -- use weight, size, and style to differentiate headings from body.
- **Don't pair similarly looking fonts** -- if two fonts are too alike, there's no reason to use both.
- **Combine serifs with sans serifs** -- this is the most reliable pairing strategy.
- **Keep it legible** -- body text must be comfortable to read at small sizes.
- **Keep it simple** -- two fonts is usually enough. Three is the maximum.
