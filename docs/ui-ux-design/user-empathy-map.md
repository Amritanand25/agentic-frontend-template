# User Empathy Map Template

> **User empathy map is a collaborative visualization technique that enables product designers to define and communicate information about the user: what they are thinking, saying, doing and feeling as they interact with a product.**

This exercise can be done collectively and helps teams better understand user personas and set the stage for creating customer journey maps.

## The Quadrants

### Think & Feel

User's goals, thoughts, hopes, fears, concerns and aspirations.

- What are they worried about?
- What matters most to them?
- What are their aspirations and dreams?

### See

What users see around themselves? What solutions they have already seen?

- What does their environment look like?
- What are their friends and colleagues doing?
- What competing products are they exposed to?

### Hear

What users hear about similar products? What sounds surround them?

- What are friends, family, and influencers telling them?
- What media channels influence them?
- What do they hear about your product or competitors?

### Pains

Challenges and obstacles in their flow. Negative emotions.

- What frustrates them?
- What obstacles stand between them and their goals?
- What risks do they fear?

### Gains

What users hope to achieve? Positive outcomes.

- What does success look like for them?
- What would make their life easier?
- What are they trying to accomplish?

---

## Practical Tips

- User empathy maps can capture one particular user or a group of users classified by a certain criterion.
- Use user's quotes about what they're thinking.
- Build the map based on data collected from user research.
- Communicate the map with stakeholders, collect different perspectives, iterate.
