# Google Design Sprint

> **A design process that takes 5 days and addresses certain UX problems through defining a problem, brainstorming concepts, prototyping and validating a design solution.**

Google Design Sprint was invented at GV (formerly Google Ventures).

---

## The 5-Day Sprint

### Monday -- Understand

Map out the problem and pick an important place to focus.

- Define the challenge
- Interview experts
- Set a long-term goal
- Map the user journey
- Pick a target area

### Tuesday -- Sketch

Sketch competing solutions on paper. Begin planning the customer test.

- Review existing ideas and inspiration
- Each team member sketches solutions individually
- Focus on detail and concrete ideas
- Begin recruiting test users

### Wednesday -- Decide

Make difficult decisions and turn your ideas into a testable hypothesis. Create a storyboard.

- Present and critique sketches
- Vote on the best solutions
- Create a step-by-step storyboard
- Plan the prototype

### Thursday -- Prototype

Build a high-fidelity prototype. Write an interview script.

- Build a realistic facade of the solution
- Focus on the customer-facing surface
- Prepare the interview script for Friday
- Do a trial run

### Friday -- Test

Test the prototype with real live humans. Analyze the results.

- Conduct 5 user interviews
- Observe and take notes
- Identify patterns
- Plan next steps

---

## Sprint Deliverables

- Answers to the posed questions
- Flow diagrams, IA maps, storyboards, empathy maps
- Prototypes
- User testing reports
- A plan for the next steps

---

## When to Use Design Sprints

- When you're planning to launch a new product, service or a feature
- Adopting a new platform or environment
- Reiterating and improving an MVP or a product
